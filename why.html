Having higher literacy rates not only benefits the individual's life but the society as a collective. Below are three specific ways where literacy gives an advantage. All the research comes from Plan International UK and HealthLine

-Being Literate Highers Your Chances of Leaving Poverty

Not being to effectively communicate with language highly decreases your chances of getting a job. Those who are illiterate or have limited resources with a library tend to be in poverty. One of the most sought skills in the job market is effectively communicating.

-Reading Changes Your Mind

Research from MRI scans proves that reading works many different circuits in your brain. Reading improves your vocabulary and can slow cognitive decline due to age.

-Higher Literacy Leads to Lower Infant Mortality Rates

Countries with high illiterate rates tend to have higher infant mortality rates due to the lack of accessible health information. Having literate and educated people decreases the rates of infant mortality and poverty.
